> International Women in Communication (IWIC) PRESENTS: 
> 
> CYBERLAW AND CYBERLIBERTIES -- A DEBATE IN PROGRESS 
>   
> Are gibes against corporations fair play on message-boards, 
> or can the poster or Internet Service Provider be sued for 
> defamation?  What if the poster is in one country, and the 
> company is based in another? 
>   
> What are corporations faced with cybersmear (Internet based 
> defamation) doing to protect their images and their profits? 
> What are Internet Service Providers' responsibilities in 
> providing the names of anonymous posters to companies? 
> Must they inform the posters that their names are being 
> provided? What about freedom of speech rights under American 
> law? 
>   
> Pauline C. Reich, Associate Professor at Waseda University 
> School of Law and Uvae Chairman of the American Chamber of 
> Commerce in Japan, will address how U.S. corporations and 
> courts are dealing with cybersmear issues.  Ms Reich is a 
> U.S. licensed attorney and co-editor (with Anderson Mori 
> partner Akira Kawamura) of the forthcoming "Law and Business 
> in Japan" and the forthcoming "Civil Procedure in Japan" 
> (with Prof. Yasuhei Taniguchi and Judge Hiroto Miyake. 
> Professor Reich teaches Internet for legal, business and 
> government research and Cyberlaw at the Waseda University 
> Graduate School of Asia Pacific Studies. (see below for a 
> list of some general cyberlaw sites)
