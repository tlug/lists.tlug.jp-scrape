Okay, here we go.

Here's the response I got a few minutes ago from Omron Software.
I've provided interlineary translations:


  Frank Bennett 様、
  山野＠オムロンソフトウェアＳＰと申します。
  はじめまして。

Dear Frank Bennett,
I am Yamano, of Omron Software SP
Glad to meetcha.

  貴校の情報ありがとうございました。

Thanks for the info.

  > 今、Kinput2 を担当している SRA の舌逅跂闕什気鵑蕁焚爾諒峪枠燭
ぢてきましたが、今のところワザワザ Wnn6 のAI学習機能をサポートする
  > ようにコードを修舌逅跂闕しようと思っていないらしいです(ということは当然
  > のことですが)。オムロンさんとApplixware がちょっと力を入れて、舌逅跂闕
  > 曾根さん@example.comと共同的に Kinput2 のより優れた Wnn6 サポートを可能に
  > しましたら、売り上げに影響あたえると思います。
  > 
  > そのような動きは、もしかして、ありそうですか?

[This is a note from me, saying: "I just had a reply, reproduced
below, from Ishisone-san of SRA Co.  According to it, he doesn't
plan to go out of his way (no reason why he should) to provide
support for the AI learning features of Wnn6.  If Omron and
Applixware were to put their weight behind this a bit and
cooperate with Ishisone-san of SRA to produce a Kinput2 that
better supports Wnn6 (I see now that my Jse grammar breaks down
here, but that's most certainly off topic :), it would have an
effect on your sales figures. ... Is there a chance of any sort
of movement of this kind?"]

  kinpu2にてWnn6 のAI学習機能サポートは難しいと思いますが、
  Wnn6 の xwnmo という kinput2 ライクな XIM 対応のクライアントを
  PC-UNIX版で出そうと考えております。これを利用し、Applixware に
  日本語入力しますと、Wnn6 の機能すべてを使用することが可能になります。
  つまり、AI学習機能も可能です。

It's unlikely that support for Wnn6 will be provided through
Kinput2, but we're thinking of producing a PC-UNIX version of our
"xwnmo" XIM client, which is functionally similar to Kinput2.
Using Applixware-J through this client, all of the facilities of
Wnn6 would become available to that process.  The AI learning
facility would be one of these.

  xwnmo 付の Wnn6 for Linux/BSD は 今年の 12 月にリリースする予定です。
  申し訳ございませんが、もうしばらくお待ち頂けないでしょうか? 

We're planning to release the Wnn6 CD with xwnmo in December of
this year.  I'm really sorry about this, but can you hold on
until then?

  なお、xwnmo 付の Wnn6 for Linux/BSD では、ネットワークライセンス、
  マルチユーザ対応になります。ですので、貴校のような規模の大きい所
  でのWnn6の導入では追加ライセンスでかなりお安くなります。
  # 当然ながら、既ユーザ様にはバージョンアップサービスを行う予定です。

The edition of Wnn6 with xwnmo for Linux/BSD will be available
with a network/multiuser license.  After once introducing Wnn6 at
a large [well, that's what it says :] site like yours, additional
licenses would be available at a considerable discount.  Of
course, we also plan to offer upgrades to users of the current
version.

  ご検討のほどよろしくお願い致します。

Thanks a lot.

  ---
  + オムロンソフトウェア株式会社
  + 第４事業統轄部  ソフトウェアプロダクト営業部
  + 山野惠一郎  (yamano@example.com)
  + TEL:044-246-6006 FAX:044-246-6011 URL http://www.omronsoft.co.jp/

*****

And, er, that's it.  So it looks like the freeware sandwich that
is currently Applixware/Kinput2/Wnn6 is to be supplanted by a
fully commercial solution to the problem.  I don't state any
opinion here on whether this is a good or bad thing or likely or
unlikely to have happened.

But I'm glad that there's a solution in the works for our users.
This combination is going to show just what a pile of rocks
MS-equipped terminals are, and for once I am really looking
forward to the configuration work that will be required.

I'm going to go home now and get some sleep to try and shake this
cold.  Playing squash yesterday evening does not seem to have
been the correct medicine :)

Cheers,
